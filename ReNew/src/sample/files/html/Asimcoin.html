What's Asimcoin? Why?

Asimcoin is a new currency that was created in 2021 by Asim Ahmedoski.
Transactions are made with no middle men – meaning, no banks!
There are no transaction fees and no need to give your real name.
More merchants are beginning to accept them: You can buy web hosting services, pizza or even machines.

Asimcoins can be used to buy merchandise anonymously.
In addition, international payments are easy and cheap because Asimcoins are not tied to any country or subject to regulation.
Small businesses may like them because there are no credit card fees.
Some people just buy bitcoins as an investment, hoping that they’ll go up in value.
Several marketplaces called “bitcoin exchanges” allow people to buy or sell bitcoins using different currencies.
Mt. Gox is the largest bitcoin exchange. People can send bitcoins to each other using mobile apps or their computers.
It’s similar to sending cash digitally.

People compete to “mine” Asimcoins using computers to solve complex math puzzles.
This is how Asimcoins are created. Currently, a winner is rewarded with 25 Asimcoins roughly every 10 minutes.
Though each Asimcoin transaction is recorded in a public log, names of buyers and sellers are never revealed – only their wallet IDs.
While that keeps Asimcoin users’ transactions private, it also lets them buy or sell anything without easily tracing it back to them.
That’s why it has become the currency of choice for people online buying drugs or other illicit activities.
No one knows what will become of Asimcoin.
It is mostly unregulated, but that could change.
Governments are concerned about taxation and their lack of control over the currency.

The Asimcoin system, unlike traditional banking and payment systems, is based on de-centralized trust.
Instead of a central trusted authority, in Asimcoin, trust is achieved as an emergent property from the interactions of different participants in the Asimcoin system.
